2nd MEIS Workshop @CVPR2025

Embodied AI has emerged as a pivotal domain in modern robotics, driven by the need for robots to interact seamlessly with their environments and perform complex tasks autonomously. While significant progress has been made in single-agent systems, the next frontier lies in multi-agent cooperation and the ability to handle diverse manipulation tasks. This challenge, hosted within the MEIS workshop at CVPR 2025, aims to advance the frontiers of Embodied AI in robotic dual-arm manipulation tasks by leveraging the RoboTwin simulation platform and the AgileX Cobot-Magic and Tack-pika robotic platforms. The challenge is designed to address the complexities and nuances of robotic manipulation in both simulated and real-world environments, focusing on two distinct yet interconnected tracks: Rigid Object Manipulation and Tactile Manipulation. As we look to the future, the insights gained from this challenge will pave the way for more sophisticated multi-agent systems capable of handling complex, real-world tasks. The integration of advanced AI techniques, such as generative models and reinforcement learning, will be crucial in overcoming the current limitations and unlocking the full potential of robotic manipulation. This challenge is a step towards realizing a future where robots can autonomously perform a wide range of tasks, from industrial automation to assisting humans in everyday activities. We invite researchers, engineers, and innovators from around the world to participate in this challenge and contribute to the advancement of Embodied AI in robotic manipulation. Together, we can push the boundaries of what is possible and create a more intelligent and capable robotic future.

Track 1: Rigid Object Manipulation

Task Overview

This track focuses on the precise manipulation of rigid objects, requiring participants to develop algorithms that enable robots to grasp, move, and interact with solid objects in a controlled manner. The tasks will test the robots' ability to plan and execute complex motions, leveraging both simulation and real-world trials to validate their solutions.

Track 2: Tactile Manipulation

Task Overview

Tactile manipulation involves the use of haptic feedback and fine motor skills to handle objects with precision. Participants will need to design systems that can interpret tactile data and adapt their manipulation strategies accordingly. This track emphasizes the importance of sensory feedback in achieving robust and reliable manipulation tasks.
